ABOUT PEPEHA

Pepeha is a traditional Māori introduction that identifies who you are and where you come from. It is a way to connect with your ancestral roots and express your identity.

In a pepeha, you typically mention your name, your family name, your ancestral mountain, river, and other significant landmarks that hold cultural importance to you.

By sharing your pepeha, you honour your heritage and contribute to the preservation of Māori culture.

History and Evolution of Pepeha

Pepeha, or "who am I?", is a fundamental concept in Māori culture that dates back to ancient times. It's a way of introducing oneself and connecting with others, rooted deeply in Māori philosophy and worldview.

Impact of Colonisation

With the arrival of European settlers, known as Pākehā, Māori culture faced significant challenges. Many Māori were forced to adapt their traditional practices to fit European norms. However, pepeha continued to play a vital role in maintaining Māori identity and connection to their heritage.

Modern Uses of Pepeha

  1. Educational Tool: Pepeha is now widely taught in schools as a way to introduce students to Māori culture and language.
  2. Workplace Integration: Many New Zealand workplaces incorporate pepeha as a way to promote cross-cultural understanding and respect.
  3. Community Building: Pepeha is used in community events and gatherings to foster connection among participants.
  4. Cultural Preservation: Efforts are made to teach and practice traditional pepeha structures to preserve Māori cultural knowledge.

The Importance of Pepeha in NZ Culture

  • Identity Expression: It allows individuals to express their unique Māori identity and connect with their heritage.
  • Relationship Building: Pepeha facilitates deeper, more meaningful interactions between people from different backgrounds.
  • Cultural Preservation: By continuing to use and teach pepeha, Māori are able to maintain and pass on their cultural practices.
  • Cross-Cultural Understanding: It serves as a bridge between Māori and non-Māori cultures, promoting greater understanding and empathy.
